# FarmWise Local Agronomy Guide

This is the ONLY source of treatment knowledge the Crop Advisor agent is allowed
to use. Every recommendation given to a farmer must trace back to an entry in
this file. If a problem is not covered here, or looks serious, it must be
escalated to a human extension officer — never guessed at.

---

## MAIZE

### Yellowing leaves (general)
- **Likely cause:** Nitrogen deficiency, especially on older/lower leaves showing
  a yellow V-shaped pattern from the tip inward.
- **Recommended action:** Apply a nitrogen-rich fertilizer (e.g. urea or NPK
  20-10-10) at the recommended local rate, split into two applications —
  3 weeks and 6 weeks after planting.
- **Escalate if:** Yellowing is accompanied by stunted growth across the whole
  field, streaking patterns, or rapid spread within days — this may indicate
  Maize Streak Virus, which requires officer confirmation.

### Maize Streak Virus (MSV)
- **Signs:** Fine yellow/white streaks running parallel to leaf veins, stunted
  plants, poor cob formation. Spread by leafhoppers.
- **Recommended action:** SEVERE / ESCALATE — no home treatment. Requires
  extension officer confirmation and possible replanting with resistant
  varieties.

### Fall Armyworm
- **Signs:** Ragged holes in leaves, sawdust-like droppings in the whorl,
  visible caterpillars.
- **Recommended action:** Early/light infestation — hand-pick larvae, apply
  wood ash or neem-based mixture into the whorl.
- **Escalate if:** Infestation covers most of the field or is spreading fast —
  may require approved pesticide application under officer guidance.

## CASSAVA

### Cassava Mosaic Disease
- **Signs:** Mottled yellow-green pattern on leaves, leaf distortion, stunted
  stems.
- **Recommended action:** SEVERE / ESCALATE — remove and destroy infected
  plants, source certified disease-free cuttings for replanting. Requires
  officer guidance before uprooting.

### Slow growth / poor tuber formation
- **Likely cause:** Poor soil fertility or waterlogging.
- **Recommended action:** Improve drainage; apply well-rotted manure or
  compost before next planting cycle; avoid planting in waterlogged plots.

## TOMATO

### Leaf curl / yellowing with stunted growth
- **Likely cause:** Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus, spread by whiteflies.
- **Recommended action:** SEVERE / ESCALATE — infected plants should be
  removed; officer guidance needed on whitefly control before replanting.

### Blossom end rot
- **Signs:** Dark, sunken patch at the bottom of the fruit.
- **Likely cause:** Calcium deficiency, often linked to inconsistent watering.
- **Recommended action:** Maintain even soil moisture; apply calcium-rich
  amendment (e.g. crushed eggshell compost or agricultural lime) to soil.

## COWPEA (BEANS)

### Pod borer damage
- **Signs:** Small holes in pods, visible larvae, reduced seed count.
- **Recommended action:** Light infestation — apply neem-based spray at
  flowering and podding stages.
- **Escalate if:** Damage is widespread across the field.

### Bacterial blight
- **Signs:** Water-soaked spots on leaves turning brown, leaf curling.
- **Recommended action:** SEVERE / ESCALATE — remove infected plant debris,
  officer guidance needed on resistant varieties for next season.

## GENERAL RULES FOR ALL CROPS

1. Any disease/pest marked **SEVERE / ESCALATE** must never be self-treated —
   always route to a human extension officer.
2. Any report of infestation or disease "spreading fast," "across the whole
   field," or "most of the plants" must be escalated regardless of the crop.
3. If the farmer's description does not clearly match an entry above, do not
   invent a diagnosis — ask a clarifying question or escalate.
